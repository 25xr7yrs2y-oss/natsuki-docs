# Official Source Map and Legal Index

This page maps the main official sources used in the EU customs legal knowledge base. It is an index for source collection and future updates.

Source check date: 2026-06-11.

## EU Legal Texts

Use EUR-Lex and ELI links for binding EU legal texts. Use CELLAR SPARQL for bulk metadata.

Core sources:

- [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/)
- [CELLAR SPARQL endpoint](https://publications.europa.eu/webapi/rdf/sparql)
- [UCC](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2013/952/oj/eng), CELEX 32013R0952.
- [UCC Delegated Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_del/2015/2446/oj/eng), CELEX 32015R2446.
- [UCC Implementing Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg_impl/2015/2447/oj/eng), CELEX 32015R2447.
- [Common Customs Tariff and CN base regulation](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/1987/2658/oj/eng), CELEX 31987R2658.

## Member State Sources

Member State sources are supplementary for penalties, appeals, VAT payment, national portals, customs broker registration, audits and practical procedures.

Examples include [Ireland Revenue customs](https://www.revenue.ie/en/customs/index.aspx), [French customs](https://www.douane.gouv.fr/), [German customs](https://www.zoll.de/), and the [Dutch Tax and Customs Administration](https://www.belastingdienst.nl/).

## Local Research Source Files

The research source base used to write this wiki is stored outside the repository in the working directory. The repository wiki intentionally contains readable pages rather than raw research dumps.

Important local files include `eu_customs_research_report.md`, `eu_customs_expansion_report.md`, `eu_customs_legal_index.csv`, `eu_customs_expanded_legal_index.csv`, `eu_customs_source_portals.csv`, and `eu_customs_expanded_source_portals.csv`.

## Update Practice

Refresh static legal metadata through CELLAR SPARQL and EUR-Lex. Refresh dynamic measures through TARIC, QUOTA, Access2Markets, sanctions, TRACES, Safety Gate and product-specific portals.
